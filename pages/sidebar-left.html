November(Ongoing)
  • Shopforchange.today

    is a tie up through Gudville with Flipkart, Amazon and Snapdeal. Through a tie-up with gudville, we decided to take advantage of the online e-commerce craze taking India by storm by publicizing shopforchange.today, where an online shopper can donate 1% of their total bill to Patched, thereby creating change as well as leveraging the power of media, to bring about pure magic. Watch how you too can shop for change.

31st October 2014
  • Ice Bucket Challenge

    Patched and Parivartan kicked off the new year with the IBC, where we had been impressed with recent efforts to prevent misuse of water while taking the challenge, and took a conscious decision to have a good time at the beach, as well as ensuring every member of the team donates to further research efforts for the ALS (Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis) association.

8th March 2014
  • Women's Day event

    An awareness drive was conducted in college to make students and staff well aware about the community needs and their support. We initiated an online and offline campaign called #WontBowDownTo to emphasize on human rights.
January 2014
  • Pop Up Store and fabric donation drive

    The Patched team organised a one-day store (popularly known as a pop-up store). Having decked up the last gazebo in college with bright patchwork miscellany, we conducted a fabric donation drive, awareness drive, product showcase, and support drive. It was a huge success with a collection of 20 kg of fabric, Rs. 5,000 in cash and massive support on our Gudville page.
  • Awareness Campaign

    Gudville is a Bangalore-based company that offers cash grants to non-profits that garner maximum supporters in a month. Patched competed with well-established NGOs with campaigns running over two months, and stood third in the January 2014 edition, scoring a cash grant of Rs. 20,000, with 1502 supporters, garnered in only 24 days. The team conducted a massive drive spanning colleges all over India, outside of India, restaurants, cafes and buildings spreading awareness for our cause! We feel humbled by the support shown towards our cause.
24th December 2013
  • Photography workshop

    Patched Project Head is also a professional photographer. She shared her tips, insight and techniques for photography in an inspiring photography-heritage-walk around the charming South Bombay area from Flora Fountain to Gateway of India, for a mere Rs. 100 registration fee. Participants felt thoroughly enriched by the knowledge and also supported our cause. The workshop raised 200% of its fundraising goal.
13th September 2013
  • Lions Club event

    Honoured to have our efforts recognized by Lions Club Auxiliary uptown. They donated two sewing machines and two stapler type sewing machines. We also held a pop-up store at the event and got an order for 200 patchwork bags from them to be used as part of their green campaign to avoid plastic.
